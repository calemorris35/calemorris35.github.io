Tangerine

Toshi Omagari is the principal designer who created Tangerine. Tangerine is a calligraphic typeface that grew inspiration revolving around the 16th and 17th century style of chancery cursive. The most distinct characteristic for the font is its tall ascender that takes a wide line space between lines resulting in a graceful texture (Tangerine). Toshi named the font after a women who encouraged him to begin his work. The elegant curves and structure of the Tangerine while creating a soft message is the reason I chose this font.
